Using AI every day exerts a quiet pull on identity. Nobody decides to sound like the model. It happens one comfortable draft at a time, which is exactly what makes it worth guarding against on purpose.

## The drift is gradual and comfortable

A model returns the average by default. Work alongside it daily and its phrasings start to feel like yours, its framings become your first reach, and the distance between your voice and its center closes a few degrees at a time. Each individual draft reads fine. That is the trap.

The same slow motion runs on identity that runs on voice. The gap between who you were conditioned to become and who you actually are is the standing subject of [Conditioned vs Authentic Identity](/concepts/conditioned-vs-authentic-identity), and a fluent tool that always has an answer is a new and powerful source of conditioning. Willpower guards this poorly, because the drift is pleasant and resolve is least available exactly when the easy option arrives.

## The Wardrobe: wear the mode, keep the self

The first safeguard is structural, not effortful. Treat the roles and registers AI helps you run as a wardrobe. Different situations call for different energies, and you put one on like a jacket, let it steer while it is on, then take it off cleanly.

The taking off is the part that does the work. A jacket worn on purpose extends you. A jacket you forget you are wearing becomes you. Applied to AI, this lets you use the model's modes, its analytical voice, its brainstorming sprawl, its polished register, without fusing with any of them. The self underneath stays whole because you keep proving, daily, that it is separable from the role.

## The Anti-Vision: draw the lines before the pressure

The second safeguard names the lines in advance. An anti-vision is the set of things you commit never to do, encoded as a standing rule rather than a decision made in the moment.

Structure guards these lines because willpower will not. The tempting-but-wrong option, the shortcut that reads fine, the framing that is not yours but is easier, arrives fast and fluent, and it arrives precisely when you have the least resolve to refuse it. A line drawn ahead of time holds under that pressure. A line you were planning to hold by feel does not.

## Keep the human as the source

Both safeguards rest on one posture: the human is the source and AI is the collaborator. Read the model as a mirror that reflects your own material rather than an engine that authors your direction, a distinction worked out in [AI Is a Mirror, Not an Engine](/concepts/ai-is-a-mirror-not-an-engine).

Held that way, daily AI use sharpens the self instead of sanding it.
