Every new AI tool promised to help. Somewhere in the collecting, the practice came apart. Notes in one app, drafts in another, tasks in a third, and no single place that holds the whole of what you are doing. The repair is worth making, because a fragmented practice spends you even when you are not working.

## The real cost is the decision tax

The monthly fees are the visible cost and the small one. The real drain is the decision tax: the constant, low-grade cost of choosing which tool to open, remembering where a given thing lives and reconciling versions that quietly drifted apart across the stack.

Each individual decision is tiny. Paid hundreds of times a day, the tax is enormous, and at solo scale nobody absorbs it for you. There is no ops team routing the work to the right home. The choosing is yours, all day, in attention you never get back. The foundation that ends the sprawl, your files, your machine, your terms, is [Data Sovereignty](/concepts/data-sovereignty).

## Move one: consolidate onto a foundation you own

Repair starts by giving the practice one home instead of a dozen. A foundation you own, rather than a scatter of rented platforms, means the work stops living in pieces across services that do not talk and offer no guarantee they will persist.

Owned does not mean fewer features. It means one reliable center of gravity that the rest can feed, so the answer to where does this live stops being a question you have to decide.

## Move two: route capture through one second brain

Ideas arrive everywhere, on a walk, mid-call, at the desk. Fragmentation happens when each arrival point becomes its own storage. The fix is a single second brain that catches from anywhere and lands everything in one place. The system that holds your thoughts so you do not have to is [The AI Second Brain](/concepts/ai-second-brain).

One capture path means one place to look later. That alone dissolves most of the reconciling tax, because there are no longer four half-versions to merge.

## Move three: let structure hold the repair

A stack cleaned up by effort will scatter again the first busy week. The repair sticks only when structure holds it, when the arrangement runs on defaults rather than on your remembering to maintain it. That principle is [Held by Structure](/concepts/held-by-structure).
