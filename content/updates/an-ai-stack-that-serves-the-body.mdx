Most AI stacks are built to produce more, faster. Almost none are built to keep the person running them well. That gap is worth closing, because a depleted operator is the one thing no automation replaces.

## The body is not a status check

It is easy to treat the body as something you audit at the end of a sprint. Tired now, fine later. That framing misses what the body actually is.

The body is closer to an improvised composition than a gauge. It knows your capacity before the conscious mind forms a sentence about it, and it plays that knowledge all day: the lift when the work is right, the drag when it is not, the quiet report on how much is genuinely left. That report is information from the largest context window you own, the one a model has never seen. The same distinction, applied to insight rather than energy, lives in [AI Is a Mirror, Not an Engine](/concepts/ai-is-a-mirror-not-an-engine).

## Most stacks override the body instead of reading it

A stack built purely for output treats the body as an obstacle to route around. Push through the drag. Fill the freed hour with more. The system measures what you produced and stays silent on what it cost, so the cost compounds unseen until it arrives all at once.

An AI stack that serves the body does the opposite. It reads capacity into the design. Capture meets you where your energy actually is, so an idea does not cost you presence to keep. A practice built exactly for that, keeping you present to the work while honoring the ideas that arrive, is [Sovereign Capture](/concepts/sovereign-capture). Pace follows the composition rather than fighting it. Automation aims at the draining work, so removing depletion is the point rather than a side effect.

## Aim automation at depletion, not just volume

The common instinct is to point automation at output: more posts, more drafts, more hours reclaimed and refilled. Point it instead at what drains you. The formatting, the chasing, the mechanical overhead that feeds nothing.

Cleared away, your attention returns to the work that actually feeds you, and the difference shows up in the work itself. Output built on a sustained human reads differently from output squeezed out of a depleted one. ## The output improves as a byproduct

Build the stack around the human and the work gets more alive, not less, because it comes from someone who is. That is the whole trade. Protect the capacity and the quality follows, since quality was always downstream of a person doing well.
